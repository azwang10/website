About Me

Hi! I'm Albert, and I'm a 3rd-year Biochemistry major at UCLA.

I love the idea that life is just a complex chemical reaction, not that different from fire, that has the potential to be completely understood at an atomic level. I think one of the end goals of biology should be to know where each atom is a cell is, what type it is, and how that atom contributes to making that cell alive.

I love how amazingly large of a challenge this is. Even with the recent accomplishments in producing the smallest viable genome, one-third of all protein coding genes within that genome have unknown functions. It pretty incredible that already have the ability to write complete codes for life with DNA synthesis but that we still don't totally know what to write yet. In essense, we're copying and pasting code but haven't yet learned how it works.

For course, to learn how sequences produce desired functions requires an enormous amount of manpower. At the moment, if protein structure is the best way to determine protein function and structure can only be detemined by individually crystallizing proteins, the rate of the determination of function for new proteins may never increase by much.

I am really excited then by the potentials of homology modeling and new assays and computational methods to quicken the discovery of the functions of sequences. Understanding how the code of life works, I think, is a big step in thinking about life as no different from any other chemical phenomenon.
